GUTTER ENERGY

Hydroelectric energy produced under your feet

Why gutter energy?

The conception of Gutter Energy was created by three physicists in Oslo. Living in a city where rain happens on a very regular occasion, and where the city river Akerselva runs freely throughout the city, they started discussing why the hydroelectrical energy potential was not utilized at all. By using Norway’s highly developed hydroelectrical technology, they have already implemented turbines throughout the sewers of the country’s most profound rain city – Bergen.

How gutter energy works?

In Norway heavy rain is normal, and a tremendous amount of water must be led through gutters and through sewers before it reaches either a river or the ocean. By utilizing the immense powers of the water traveling below our feet, Gutter Energy has made it possible to create sustainable and green energy out of the resources that already exist in a city. The turbines installed in the sewer tunnels have a relatively low-cost production and require between low to no maintenance, as the use of strong metals will keep them spinning for tens of years.

Our goal

Our initial goal is to implement Gutter Energy to every big Scandinavian city. Furthermore, we aspire to install our systems in every European city where there annually is a substantial amount of rain downfall. This would make our continent a more sustainable home for not only us, but also for animals the nature surrounding our historic cities. Cities will be more independent, with a goal of creating a minimum of 35% of electricity solely from the waters beneath our feet.

About the creators

Emil Hengy, Sony Ericsson and William James met as students at NTNU Trondheim, where they all studied to become physicists. Both Ericsson and James moved to Norway’s capitol, Oslo to start working for Green Energy directly after their studies where they quickly got to lead their own project team. Emil Hengy has worked for Sustainable Waters since one year pre-graduation. In 2017 the trident once again teamed up to discuss starting their own business based on the experience they had gathered up through the past years. This is how Gutter Energy was created, and the rest is soon to be history.
